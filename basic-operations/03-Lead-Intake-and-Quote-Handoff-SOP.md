# Lead Intake And Quote Handoff SOP

Status: Draft for internal review
Last updated: 2026-07-03
Owner: Management / assigned sales or mailbox operator
Applies to: Humans and agents handling new moving inquiries, quote requests, and lead handoffs

## Purpose

Turn every new moving inquiry into a clear, trackable lead record with enough facts for a responsible quote, call, or management review.

## Main rule

Answer quickly and professionally, gather the move facts, record the lead source, and move the lead into Supermove or the approved reporting process. Do not guess pricing, promise availability, approve discounts, or claim special services without management approval.

## Response standard

- Review new lead and quote messages promptly, same day when practical.
- For partner/local leads, same-business-day quote response is a target where practical, not an external guarantee unless management approves it.
- Convert email, text, web, Facebook, or referral inquiries toward a call when details are incomplete or the job is complex.
- Acknowledge what the customer already shared before asking for more.
- Use calm, practical value language: careful crews, clear communication, organized loading, property protection, reliable service, and fair value.

## Required intake facts

Collect these before a quote or handoff whenever possible:

| Area | Facts to collect |
| --- | --- |
| Customer | Name, phone, email, best callback window. |
| Timing | Move date, target window, flexibility, deadline, closing/lease/elevator constraints. |
| Origin | Pickup address or ZIP, property type, stairs/elevators/long carries/loading dock/parking/building rules. |
| Destination | Delivery address or ZIP if known, property type, access constraints, building rules. |
| Scope | Full home, apartment, office, single item, labor-only, packing/unpacking, added stops. |
| Inventory | Room-by-room inventory or item list; heavy, fragile, sentimental, high-value, awkward, or difficult items. |
| Protection/access | Floor/wall/elevator protection needs, tight hallways, weather concerns, COI/building requirements. |
| Packing | Customer-packed vs packing help needed; boxes/materials; disassembly/reassembly needs if known. |
| Source | How the customer heard about Dos Manos; referral partner/person; online/ad/page/source tag when known. |
| Status | Ready to book, comparing quotes, gathering information, waiting on date/building details, or needs management review. |

## Lead intake process

1. Identify the lead source: Gmail, phone, form, social, referral, partner, repeat customer, Google/GBP, or other channel.
2. Respond or draft a response that confirms the inquiry and asks for missing move facts.
3. If the customer gives enough detail for a quote process, hand off to the approved estimator/manager or enter it in Supermove if authorized.
4. If the lead is incomplete, request a short call or the missing essentials.
5. If the job may involve pricing exceptions, specialty service, interstate moves, insurance/COI, unusual building requirements, damage/claim history, or high-risk items, escalate before quoting or promising.
6. Record lead source and next follow-up date.
7. Keep the lead visible until it is booked, declined, delegated, or closed under management rules.

## Quote-to-call handoff

Move the lead toward a call when:

- The inventory is vague.
- Origin/destination or access details are missing.
- The customer asks for a firm price from incomplete facts.
- The job has stairs, elevators, long carries, building rules, COI requirements, specialty/heavy/fragile/sentimental items, multiple stops, packing needs, or a tight deadline.
- The customer seems worried, frustrated, comparison-shopping, or unsure.

Do not pressure the customer. The call should make the quote more accurate and reduce surprises.

## Supermove handoff

Supermove is the central job-management record whenever possible. Required lead fields from current policy defaults are:

- Customer name.
- Phone.
- Email.
- Pickup address / ZIP.
- Delivery address / ZIP, if known.
- Move date or target window.
- Move type.
- Lead source.
- Referring person / partner, if any.
- Assigned operator / participant, if any.
- Lead status.
- Notes on job size/scope.
- Next follow-up date.

Before booking, the approved record should also show:

- Written estimate completed/sent.
- Estimate accepted.
- Crew/truck plan.
- Pricing/rate/minimum confirmed under approved policy.
- Inventory/scope notes.
- Specialty items.
- Access issues.
- Deposit/payment terms.
- Customer documents sent/signed.
- Source/attribution confirmed.
- Pricing exception approval, if applicable.

If the person handling the lead cannot enter Supermove directly, they must notify management the same day with the same information.

## Guardrails

Do not:

- Invent pricing, minimum hours, discounts, materials charges, travel rules, deposit rules, or specialty-item charges.
- Promise crew size, truck size, arrival windows, final cost, discount, claim outcome, COI turnaround, or service availability unless management approved it.
- Claim unverified services such as piano moving, antiques, storage, same-day availability, guarantees, or other specialty capabilities.
- Book interstate household goods moves unless management confirms FMCSA/U.S. DOT authority and required documents.
- Promise referral terms, W-9 handling, payout timing, or partner approval without management.
- Move lead/customer details into private side lists outside approved systems.

## Draft templates

### First response / call setup

```text
Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out to Dos Manos Moving. I can help get the details so we can give you the right next step.

What date are you looking to move, what are you moving, and where are pickup and delivery? If it is easier, we can handle the details on a quick call.
```

### Missing details request

```text
Thanks, that helps. To make sure the quote matches the job, can you also send:

- pickup and delivery ZIPs or addresses;
- stairs, elevators, long carries, parking, or building rules;
- a quick inventory or room list;
- any heavy, fragile, sentimental, or difficult items;
- whether you need packing help;
- the best phone number and callback window?
```

### Internal quote handoff note

```text
Lead source: [Gmail / phone / form / referral / Google / social / other]
Customer: [name + approved contact route]
Move date/window: [date]
Origin/destination: [ZIPs/addresses in approved system]
Scope/inventory: [summary]
Access/building notes: [stairs/elevator/parking/COI/etc.]
Special items/risks: [summary]
Customer status: [ready / comparing / needs call / missing info]
Next follow-up date: [date]
Approval issues: [pricing exception / COI / interstate / specialty / claim / none]
```

### Helpful follow-up

```text
Hi [Name], this is [Name] with Dos Manos Moving. I wanted to follow up on your move for [move date]. Do you have any questions, or would you like us to confirm the next step?
```

## Quality checks

Before closing a lead intake item, confirm:

- [ ] Required move facts were collected or missing facts were requested.
- [ ] Lead source/referral source was recorded.
- [ ] Next follow-up date or owner is clear.
- [ ] The lead is visible in Supermove, the approved record, or the correct queue.
- [ ] No unauthorized price, availability, service, claim, COI, referral, or legal commitment was made.
- [ ] Customer-sensitive details stayed inside approved systems.

## Escalation

Escalate before quoting or booking when a lead involves pricing exceptions, unclear scope, high-risk/heavy/specialty items, interstate moves, COI/additional insured requests, claims/customer disputes, payment problems, public-review threats, unusual building rules, or anything outside normal approved service.
