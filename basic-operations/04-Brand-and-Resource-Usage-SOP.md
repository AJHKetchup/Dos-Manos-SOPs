# Brand And Resource Usage SOP

Status: Draft for internal review
Last updated: 2026-07-03
Owner: Management / assigned brand or marketing operator
Applies to: Humans and agents drafting Dos Manos copy, selecting media, or using brand resources

## Purpose

Keep Dos Manos public and internal materials consistent with the current brand source of truth while preventing unsupported claims, unsafe media use, and accidental public edits.

## Main rule

Use approved brand resources and practical quality-focused language. Draft public-facing work for approval; do not publish or change live public surfaces without owner approval.

## Positioning

Dos Manos should be framed as:

> The Quality Moving Company: careful crews, clear communication, organized loading, customer-property protection, reliable service, and fair value.

Use Trust, Tranquility, and Professionalism as internal themes. Public copy should sound direct, practical, local, and human, not corporate, fake-luxury, bargain-basement, or hype-heavy.

## Approved language themes

Use:

- Careful crews.
- Clear communication.
- Organized loading.
- Furniture protection, wrapping, padding, and respectful handling.
- Prepared crews and real trucks.
- Apartment, condo, hallway, elevator, and building-rule realities.
- Reliable service and fair value.
- Broad service-area language such as River Grove, DuPage County, and nearby western suburbs when public copy needs geography.

Avoid:

- Cheapest-mover positioning.
- Fake urgency or scarcity.
- Unsupported guarantees.
- Unsupported claims about licensing, insurance, piano moving, antiques, storage, same-day availability, or specialty services.
- Full backend service-area city lists in public copy.
- Public language that implies independent locations, franchises, ownership, territory rights, or legal/economic promises.

## Media selection rules

Prefer:

- Real crew photos.
- Real truck and loading proof.
- Wrapped/protected furniture.
- Dolly, hallway, apartment, condo, elevator, and building-rule proof.
- Testimonial videos or review proof when approved.
- Clean before/after or process proof when privacy-safe.

Avoid or reject:

- Fake stock photos.
- Blurry, dark, low-quality, or confusing images.
- Unsafe lifting or unsafe job-site behavior.
- Customer paperwork, screens, labels, job sheets, payment info, or private documents.
- Children’s faces.
- House numbers, license plates, apartment numbers, or other private location details.
- Customer-sensitive belongings or conditions that could embarrass or identify the customer.

If privacy or quality is unclear, do not publish. Route it for owner/brand review.

## Public-edit gates

Owner approval is required before changing or publishing:

- Google Business Profile posts, services, Q&A, service areas, hours, photos, review replies, or business description.
- Website, social, ad, directory, or public listing copy.
- Public media selections.
- Referral/affiliate terms or partner-facing program claims.
- Any claim about licensing, insurance, specialty services, guarantees, availability, piano moving, antiques, storage, or similar capabilities.
- Public location/territory language.

## Drafting process

1. Identify the surface: internal SOP, Drive guide, website, GBP, social, ad, directory, sales material, partner material, or media asset.
2. Pull brand facts from the Brand Source of Truth, business operations brief, or current approved source.
3. Draft in direct, practical language with concrete service proof.
4. Remove unsupported claims, hype, fake urgency, and claims that need owner/legal/compliance confirmation.
5. Check media for privacy, safety, quality, and source status.
6. Mark the draft as internal, approval-ready, or approved. Do not imply approval if it has not been given.
7. For public surfaces, hand off for owner approval instead of publishing.
8. Record source paths and unresolved approvals in the final note or index.

## Quality checks

Before a brand or resource draft is considered ready for review, confirm:

- [ ] It uses quality, care, protection, communication, and fair-value positioning.
- [ ] It does not claim unverified services, guarantees, licensing/insurance status, or availability.
- [ ] It does not expose backend service-area settings or private customer/job details.
- [ ] It does not change referral, compensation, legal, or territory terms.
- [ ] Media, if used, has been screened for privacy, quality, and safety.
- [ ] The source path or asset location is named.
- [ ] Any required approval gate is explicit.

## Escalation

Escalate before publishing, posting, replying publicly, using uncertain media, changing brand claims, changing public location/service language, using restricted customer examples, or mentioning any claim that may require legal, insurance, licensing, regulatory, or owner confirmation.

## Automation opportunities

- Brand-copy lint checks for unsupported claims.
- Media review checklists for privacy/safety flags.
- Draft packets for GBP posts, review replies, and website updates that require owner approval before publication.
