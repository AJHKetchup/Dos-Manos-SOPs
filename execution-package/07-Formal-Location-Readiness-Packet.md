# Formal Location Readiness Packet

Internal packet for deciding whether a territory is ready to become a more separated Dos Manos location.

Use after the Territory Assignment and Launch Packet has been active long enough to produce meaningful evidence. This packet should rely on Supermove, QuickBooks/accounting records, claims/customer records, review activity, referral activity, and management observations.

## Purpose

This packet helps management decide whether a territory is ready for more investment, more separation, General Manager promotion, or location-level opportunity review.

Use this only after a territory has meaningful activity. Do not use it to promise a Formal Location, General Manager role, ownership, buy-in, cash-flow participation, or profit participation.

Working review authority: Alex, Bryan, and the bookkeeper/CPA review location-level economics. Alex and Bryan approve any General Manager promotion. Any cash-flow participation, profit participation, phantom equity, buy-in, ownership, or other economic structure requires ownership-group approval with attorney and CPA review.

Prior planning assumptions discussed for a DuPage-style location included approximately $15,000/month revenue, approximately $180,000/year revenue, approximately 20% margin, and approximately $36,000/year profit. These are planning references, not automatic readiness thresholds.

Other prior planning references include a direct labor target around 30%-35% of billed revenue, a minimum job revenue / minimum job cost around $450, and a baseline margin concept around 30% in wage/pricing discussions. These should be tested against actual Supermove and QuickBooks data before any location decision.

Working margin target: positive contribution margin after labor, truck/rental, materials, fuel, direct marketing, claims, and referral/revenue-share payouts. The labor benchmark is roughly 35% of revenue, but the final location decision should use actual territory data.

| Quality gate | Yes / No | Strong job quality, low avoidable claims, and acceptable customer feedback. |
| Operations gate | Yes / No | Reliable crew, truck, dispatch, billing, collections, and claims support. |
| Reporting gate | Yes / No | Supermove and QuickBooks records are clean enough for management review. |
| Market gate | Yes / No | Repeatable lead sources and credible local referral relationships. |
| Operator gate | Yes / No | Territory Operator is ready for Working Foreman / General Manager responsibility. |

Suggested interpretation:

- Mostly green: consider increased investment, dedicated assets, Formal Location review, or General Manager path.
- Mixed green/yellow: keep centrally managed and focus on the weakest gates.
- Any major red in trust, quality, claims, collected revenue, or compliance: do not promote until corrected.

Revenue should not override red flags in customer trust, claim history, operator judgment, collected revenue, or compliance. A busy territory is not automatically a mature territory.

## Location-Level Opportunity Review

A proven General Manager may become eligible for a location-level economic opportunity if the territory has enough performance, maturity, trust, and clean economics.

Possible structures may include:

- Cash-flow participation.
- Profit participation.
- Phantom equity.
- Buy-in opportunity.
- Location-level ownership.
- Another approved economic structure.

No ownership, equity, cash-flow participation, profit participation, buy-in opportunity, or location-level right is automatic.

Any location-level opportunity requires:

- Sustained territory performance.
- Strong job quality.
- Clean reporting.
- Low avoidable claims and complaints.
- Management approval.
- Legal review.
- Tax review.
- Accounting review.
- Written structure approved by Dos Manos.

The intended structure is location-level and does not imply repeated dilution of the parent company.

Do not use prior investor discussions, location-level equity concepts, holding-company concepts, hybrid structures, or any discussed purchase price as final terms unless they are confirmed against signed documents and approved by counsel and tax/accounting.

## Internal Acceptance Check

Management should be able to decide whether the territory is ready for more investment, more separation, General Manager promotion, or location-level opportunity review.

Before making a decision, confirm:

- Revenue history is based on collected revenue, not just booked revenue.
- Job count and lead sources are real and trackable.
- Margin review includes labor, trucks, claims, ads, and central operations burden.
- Operator readiness is based on performance, judgment, and trust.
- Central operations can support the next stage.
- Legal, tax, payroll, insurance, and franchise concerns are reviewed before any formal economic structure is offered.
- Financial assumptions have been checked against actual Supermove and QuickBooks records.
- The management decision memo is completed before any structural change is communicated.

## Final Review Items

- Counsel should review any Formal Location or location-level opportunity structure before discussion with the participant.
- Tax and accounting should review cash-flow participation, profit participation, phantom equity, buy-in, or ownership concepts before they are offered.
- Payroll should review any General Manager compensation changes.
- Insurance should review dedicated assets, operating addresses, advertised location language, and claims handling.
- Counsel should review franchise, securities, employment, confidentiality, non-solicitation, customer ownership, referral partner ownership, and territory reassignment implications.
- Tax/accounting should review truck depreciation, partnership tax treatment, Form 1065 reporting implications, and any location-level economic structure before presentation.
