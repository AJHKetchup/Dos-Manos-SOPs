# Territory Assignment and Launch Packet

Use this packet only after a participant earns a territory.

Use with the Operator Performance Tracker. A territory should not be assigned because of enthusiasm alone; it should be assigned after documented performance, judgment, and trust.

## Purpose

This packet assigns a territory, defines tracking channels, and launches the territory as an Advertised Location while operations remain centrally managed by Dos Manos.

The goal is to make the territory visible, trackable, and accountable without creating confusion about ownership, separate business rights, or operational control.

Dos Manos' first operating base is in DuPage County, and early territory planning may include DuPage-area markets, North Chicago / north-side markets, and nearby Chicago suburban markets. Territory design should protect quality and operational control before expansion speed.

Working first territory to consider: North Side / North Shore Chicago. Safer pilot alternative: a defined DuPage / West Suburbs territory if Dos Manos wants less operational expansion risk.

## Territory Boundaries

Describe the territory clearly enough for marketing, Supermove source tracking, referral activity, and management review.

Use one or more:

- Cities or suburbs.
- ZIP codes.
- Service radius. A rough 20-mile radius may be useful as a planning starting point, depending on density and operating capacity.
- Named neighborhoods.
- Counties.
- Map link or internal map reference.
- Excluded areas.

If the territory is based on expansion planning, note whether the market has a strong base of middle to upper-middle-income customers, homes above the company's target value threshold, referral density, or practical truck/crew access.

## Partner Lead Response Standards

For partner-referred leads, Dos Manos should aim for:

- Same-business-day local quote response.
- COI turnaround under 24 hours when required and practical.
- Proactive status texts when a partner referral is active.
- Same-day acknowledgment of customer or partner issues.
- Resolution within one business day where practical.

These are operating targets, not legal guarantees. If the team cannot meet one, the Territory Operator or manager should communicate the next realistic step.

## Public Launch Copy

Short version:

> Dos Manos Moving is now serving [Territory] with professional moving services backed by the same Dos Manos standard for quality, care, and reliability.

Website or social version:

> Dos Manos Moving is now serving [Territory]. Our team provides professional moving services with careful communication, reliable crews, and the Dos Manos standard for quality and care. Request a quote through our [Territory] page or call [territory phone number].

Referral partner version:

> Dos Manos Moving is now serving [Territory], and we are building referral relationships with local professionals who need a reliable moving partner for customers, residents, and clients. If someone needs careful moving help, please send them to [territory phone number] or [territory quote page] so we can track and serve them properly.

Quality-positioning version:

> Dos Manos Moving is The Quality Moving Company for [Territory]. We help customers who want careful crews, clear communication, and a move handled the right way at a fair price.

Do not use language that promises independent ownership, separate business rights, or separate operating control.

Public copy should also avoid positioning Dos Manos as the cheapest mover or as luxury-only. The preferred position is quality-focused and value-driven: careful crews, clear communication, reliable execution, and fair pricing.

Approval rule: Alex approves public copy for brand, business, and legal-risk language. Bryan approves service area and operations accuracy before launch.

## Proof And Review Language

Use proof carefully.

Allowed if true and current:

- Number or general strength of reviews.
- Specific service standards.
- Real local service areas.
- Real referral partner categories.
- Real job examples approved by management.

Do not:

- Exaggerate review count or ratings.
- Use a customer's name, employer, or high-profile job example without approval.
- Imply every crew, territory, or job has the same review history if the claim is not accurate.
- Suggest Dos Manos is luxury-only or lowest-price.
- Use testimonials or review language that violates platform rules or FTC review guidance.

## Pause, Adjust, Or Reassign Triggers

Management should consider pausing, adjusting, or reassigning a territory if:

- Territory leads are not being tracked cleanly.
- The Territory Operator is not using Supermove or reporting accurately.
- Customer communication is damaging trust.
- Pricing guidelines are not being followed.
- Avoidable damage, claims, or complaints increase.
- Referral partners receive poor response or unclear communication.
- The territory overloads dispatch, crews, trucks, billing, or claims handling.
- Public copy or local outreach creates ownership, independent-control, or separate-business confusion.
- The operator makes unapproved arrangements outside the approved Dos Manos process.
- Compliance, insurance, ICC, interstate, or COI issues are not being escalated.

Use the First 30-Day Territory Review and Operator Performance Tracker before making a final decision when practical.
