## Good Weekly Performance Looks Like

This is a coaching guide, not an automatic promotion standard.

| Area | Strong signal |
| --- | --- |
| Activity | Participant creates or advances real leads instead of waiting passively. |
| Supermove discipline | Lead sources, job notes, follow-ups, and issues are entered or reported on time. |
| Follow-up | Open quotes are followed up without pressure or spam. |
| Quality | Jobs are handled with care, documentation, and few avoidable issues. |
| Pricing | Participant follows guidelines and asks before guessing. |
| Referral work | Participant makes professional, trackable contacts with relevant local partners. |
| Accountability | Participant brings up problems early and asks for help before damage spreads. |

## Weekly Warning Signs

| Area | Warning sign |
| --- | --- |
| Activity | Participant reports vague activity without names, sources, or next steps. |
| Supermove discipline | Leads, follow-ups, or issues are missing from the approved record. |
| Follow-up | Customer follow-up is either absent or too aggressive. |
| Quality | Same types of customer issues, damage, or crew concerns repeat. |
| Pricing | Participant discounts, guesses, or avoids pricing guidelines. |
| Referral work | Participant promises referral payment before setup is approved. |
| Accountability | Participant hides problems, blames others, or waits for management to discover issues. |

## Monthly Review Agenda

Use this agenda to keep the review practical.

1. Review weekly activity totals.
2. Compare Supermove records to the participant's self-report.
3. Confirm collected revenue against QuickBooks or accounting records.
4. Review job quality, customer feedback, issues, and claims.
5. Review pricing discipline and any discounts, misquotes, or scope changes.
6. Review follow-up discipline and stale quotes.
7. Review referral partner activity.
8. Identify the biggest improvement needed before the next review.
9. Decide whether to continue, improve before promotion, assign territory, pause, or remove.
10. Set next week's priority and next review date.

## Stage Decision Communication Templates

Continue:

> We are continuing you in the Operator Track. The main focus before the next review is [priority]. Your next review date is [date].

Improve before promotion:

> You are making progress, but we are not moving you to the next stage yet. Before we reconsider, we need improvement in [specific areas], supported by [specific evidence].

Assign territory:

> Based on documented performance, judgment, and trust, Dos Manos is prepared to consider territory assignment. Territory assignment will be governed by the Territory Assignment and Launch Packet and does not create ownership, independent control, or automatic future economic rights.

Pause:

> We are pausing your participation in the program because [specific reason]. We will review whether participation can resume after [condition/date], if applicable.

Remove:

> We are removing you from the program at this time because [specific job-related reason]. This does not change any normal field work arrangement unless management separately communicates that decision.

## Internal Acceptance Check

Management should be able to look at this tracker and decide whether the participant is ready for a territory.

Before using it, management should confirm:

- The weekly reporting deadline is clear.
- The manager responsible reviews it monthly.
- Collected revenue matches QuickBooks or approved accounting records.
- Claims and customer issues are reviewed before promotion.
- Stage decisions are documented.
- Territory assignment decisions are not made without reviewing this tracker.
- Territory assignment is not contingent on a fixed number of completed jobs. The tracker should show enough evidence for Bryan to judge operator readiness, company expansion readiness, market fit, and operating capacity.

## Final Review Items

- If converted to a spreadsheet, lock formula cells and standardize date, currency, and stage-decision fields.
- Confirm who has access to performance records.
- Confirm performance records are retained according to company employment record practices.
