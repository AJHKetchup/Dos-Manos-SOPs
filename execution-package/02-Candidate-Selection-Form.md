# Candidate Selection Form

Company form for deciding whether a candidate should enter the Operator Track.

Use after the candidate has reviewed the Program Brief and before they receive the Operator Playbook as an active participant.

## Approval Roles

Bryan Abrego gives final approval on candidate acceptance, hold, or rejection unless he delegates authority in writing. Bryan Abrego is CEO, owns operational judgment, and is the default first manager responsible for Operator Track execution. Alex Heinz may support program documentation, participant communication language, tracking, and administrative setup where assigned. Luis Abrego may provide operational input when involved, but Bryan Abrego makes the final program decision unless delegated.

Selection scores are a management evaluation standard, not a legal entitlement or automatic acceptance rule. Management may hold or reject a candidate when trust, judgment, safety, customer communication, or operational concerns outweigh the numeric score.

## Scoring Guidance

Use the score as a decision aid, not as a substitute for judgment.

| Result | Suggested decision | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Any trustworthiness score below 3 | Usually reject or hold | Do not advance a candidate who creates trust concerns. |
| Any customer judgment score below 3 | Usually hold | Customer judgment is central to the Operator Track. |
| Any pricing discipline score below 3 | Usually hold | The program should not train discount-first behavior. |
| Average score below 3.0 | Usually reject or hold | Candidate is not ready for Operator Track pressure. |
| Average score 3.0-3.7 | Possible hold or limited accept | Use conditions, first review date, and close manager oversight. |
| Average score 3.8 or higher | Possible accept | Still requires manager judgment and clean minimum screen. |

Minimum screen failures should outweigh a high average score.

## Practical Interview Questions

Select 8 to 12 questions from this bank. Managers may skip questions that do not fit the candidate, but should keep the interview focused on job-related judgment.

## Manager Pre-Interview Checklist

- [ ] Reviewed candidate's current role and work history.
- [ ] Reviewed any available customer, crew, safety, and field performance notes.
- [ ] Confirmed which questions are job-related for this candidate.
- [ ] Avoiding protected-characteristic or non-job-related questions.
- [ ] Ready to explain that the Operator Track is selective and not a guarantee.
- [ ] Ready to describe Supermove, weekly reporting, and revenue share at a high level.

1. A customer calls and says they need a move this week but they are vague about how much furniture they have. How would you handle the call?
2. A customer asks for a lower price than the Dos Manos guideline. What do you say?
3. You realize during a call that the job may need more crew or truck capacity than the customer expected. How do you explain that without losing trust?
4. A lead came from someone you know personally. What steps do you take so Dos Manos can track it correctly?
5. You are leading a crew and the customer seems unhappy halfway through the move. What do you do first?
6. A crew member is rushing and may damage furniture. How do you handle it on the job?
7. A customer complains after the move about a scratch or missing item. What do you report and to whom?
8. How would you follow up with a customer who received a quote but has not booked?
9. What local businesses or referral partners could send moving leads to Dos Manos?
10. How would you introduce Dos Manos to a realtor, property manager, senior living contact, contractor, or decorator?
11. What weekly numbers would help management know whether you are making progress?
12. Tell us about a time you had to follow a system even when it felt slower than doing it your own way.
13. A customer says another mover is cheaper. How do you explain Dos Manos' value without sounding defensive?
14. What job details should be documented in Supermove before a crew arrives?
15. What would you photograph or document before moving an item that already appears damaged?
16. A referral partner asks how they get paid for sending Dos Manos a job. What would you say, and what would you ask management to confirm?

## Strong Answer Indicators

Look for answers that show:

- The candidate asks questions before quoting or reacting.
- The candidate protects the Dos Manos service standard without sounding scripted.
- The candidate understands quality is sold through specifics: crew preparation, protection, communication, and reliability.
- The candidate knows when to escalate.
- The candidate documents job facts rather than relying on memory.
- The candidate can build referral relationships without sounding pushy.
- The candidate accepts that revenue alone is not enough for promotion.

## Concern Indicators

Pause or reject the candidate if the interview shows:

- The candidate wants a territory before proving the Operator Track basics.
- The candidate treats discounting as the first answer to price pressure.
- The candidate resists Supermove or written documentation.
- The candidate minimizes damage, claims, or customer complaints.
- The candidate suggests side arrangements outside Dos Manos.
- The candidate cannot explain how they would protect the brand in front of a customer.
- The candidate avoids accountability for follow-up, reporting, or crew communication.

## Trust Red Flags

These should carry extra weight even if the candidate has strong sales or field ability.

- Blames customers or crew members without taking responsibility.
- Treats documentation as optional.
- Suggests private arrangements outside Dos Manos.
- Minimizes damage, claims, or payment problems.
- Pushes for territory, ownership, or special compensation before proving the Operator Track.
- Says what management wants to hear but cannot give practical examples.
- Shows poor judgment with customer information or company information.

## Candidate Communication Templates

Accept:

> We are accepting you into the Operator Track. This means you will start training and being evaluated on booking, customer communication, Supermove discipline, job quality, follow-up, and weekly reporting. It does not guarantee a territory or promotion. Your manager will be [Name], your start date is [Date], and your first review will be [Date].

Hold:

> We see potential, but we are not starting you in the Operator Track yet. Before reconsidering, we want to see improvement in [specific areas]. We will review again on or after [Date].

Reject:

> We are not moving forward with the Operator Track at this time. The decision is based on the current requirements for reliability, customer judgment, communication, field performance, and ability to follow the Dos Manos process.

Keep the message brief, factual, and tied to job-related criteria.

## Distribution Readiness Check

Management should be able to make a clear yes, no, or hold decision from this form.

Before accepting a candidate, confirm:

- The decision is based on job-related criteria.
- The candidate understands the Operator Track is a test, not a guarantee.
- The candidate can be managed under the weekly reporting rhythm.
- The manager responsible is named.
- The start date and first review date are clear.
- The candidate will not be treated as accepted until the decision section is complete.

## Professional Review Requirements

- Review selection criteria for employment law compliance before implementation.
- Do not ask questions about protected personal characteristics, family status, disability, age, religion, national origin, or other non-job-related matters.
- If this form is used for outside hires, confirm it aligns with Dos Manos hiring, background check, driving, employment eligibility, safety, and probationary field period procedures.
- If the candidate may drive, confirm driver qualification, insurance, safety, and vehicle-policy requirements before assigning driving responsibility.
- Confirm required household-goods movement training is complete before the candidate is used as a driver, helper, or packer on Illinois household goods shipments; any trainee should be supervised by a trained employee.
