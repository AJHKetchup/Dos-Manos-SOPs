# Operator Playbook

Participant-facing operating guide for the Operator Track.

Use after the participant is accepted into the Operator Track. The playbook should be paired with the Operator Performance Tracker so training, weekly activity, and stage decisions stay connected.

## Role Of An Operator Track Participant

An Operator Track participant is being trained and tested to bring in, book, help execute, and report quality Dos Manos jobs.

The role is to learn how to operate inside the approved Dos Manos process, not around it.

An Operator Track participant is expected to:

- Bring in and book quality leads.
- Communicate clearly with customers.
- Follow pricing guidelines.
- Use Supermove or the approved reporting process.
- Track lead sources.
- Help execute jobs under the Dos Manos service standard.
- Lead crews professionally when assigned.
- Follow up with customers.
- Build referral relationships.
- Report weekly activity.
- Ask for help before a small issue becomes a large issue.

## Dos Manos Service Standard

Dos Manos positions itself as The Quality Moving Company. Every job should protect that reputation.

The service standard means:

- Show up prepared.
- Communicate clearly before, during, and after the job.
- Protect the customer's home and belongings.
- Work efficiently without rushing carelessly.
- Follow the approved price and scope.
- Keep the customer informed if the scope changes.
- Escalate damage, claims, pricing confusion, crew issues, or customer dissatisfaction quickly.
- Leave the customer feeling respected.
- Deliver value at a fair price instead of trying to be the cheapest option.

## Booking Standards

Before a job is booked, confirm:

- Customer name.
- Phone number.
- Email.
- Move date.
- Origin address.
- Destination address.
- Move type.
- Inventory or job size.
- Stairs, elevators, long carries, parking, loading dock, or building restrictions.
- Packing needs.
- Specialty items.
- Crew and truck needs.
- Pricing guideline used.
- Deposit or booking requirement, if applicable.
- How the customer heard about Dos Manos.
- Lead source or referral source.

Do not promise crew size, truck size, arrival windows, discounts, claim outcomes, or special handling outside approved Dos Manos standards.

## Pricing Guideline Basics

Use the current Dos Manos pricing guidelines. If unsure, ask management before quoting.

Working minimum: $450 minimum job revenue / minimum job cost unless management updates the pricing guide.

Exact hourly rates, crew/truck rates, travel time, materials, stair charges, heavy-item charges, tariff-specific pricing, and recurring exception rules still need to be inserted from the current Dos Manos pricing guide.

Pricing discipline means:

- Do not guess on large or complex moves.
- Do not discount without approval.
- Do not promise a fixed final total unless management has approved that structure.
- Do not hide likely charges from the customer.
- Explain what can change the final cost, such as more inventory, access issues, extra packing, long carries, stairs, waiting time, or added stops.
- Put important quote details in Supermove or the approved written record.

Dos Manos should be positioned as quality-focused and value-driven, not as the lowest-cost mover. If a customer compares Dos Manos to a cheaper option, explain the service standard, trained crews, property protection, communication, and reliability without criticizing the other mover.

Do not say:

- "We are the cheapest."
- "We can beat any price."
- "That other mover is bad."
- "The price will definitely not change."
- "Do not worry about the details."
- "We can handle anything," if the job may require special approval, licensing, equipment, or authority.

## Customer Call Process

1. Greet the customer and identify Dos Manos.
2. Ask what they need moved and when.
3. Gather the booking details.
4. Ask how they heard about Dos Manos.
5. Explain the next step clearly.
6. Give the quote or send the estimate process to management if needed.
7. Confirm what the customer needs to do to book.
8. Enter the lead and notes into Supermove or the approved tracking process.

## Customer Conversation Standard

Good customer communication is calm, specific, and curious.

Use this sequence when the customer is uncertain, pushing on price, or frustrated:

1. Ask a real question.
2. Listen without interrupting.
3. Restate the concern in plain language.
4. Confirm the job facts.
5. Explain the Dos Manos standard or next step.
6. Document the conversation in Supermove.

Avoid:

- Arguing with the customer.
- Dropping price before understanding the real concern.
- Over-explaining when the customer needs a clear next step.
- Saying yes just to end an uncomfortable call.
- Making promises about claims, crew size, timing, or final cost without approval.
- Faking scarcity or false urgency to pressure a customer.

Useful questions:

- "What matters most for this move?"
- "What are you worried could go wrong?"
- "What did the other quote include?"
- "What would you want the crew to know before they arrive?"
- "Is there anything fragile, heavy, sentimental, or difficult that we should plan for?"

## Discovery Question Bank

Use the questions that fit the job.

Move scope:

- What are you moving?
- Is this a full home, apartment, office, single item, or specialty move?
- Are there any items that are especially heavy, fragile, sentimental, or difficult?
- Are you packing yourself, or do you need packing help?

Access:

- Are there stairs, elevators, long carries, loading docks, tight hallways, or parking limits?
- Are there building rules, certificates of insurance, elevator reservations, or time windows?
- Is there anything about either location that would surprise the crew?

Timing:

- What date do you need?
- Is the date flexible?
- Are there closing, lease, elevator, or delivery deadlines?

Decision:

- What matters most to you for this move?
- Have you worked with movers before?
- Are you comparing quotes, or are you ready to book if the details work?
- What would make you comfortable with the next step?

## Estimate Process

Use an in-person, virtual, phone-based, or written customer description process approved by management.

For every estimate, record:

- What the customer says is being moved.
- Access details.
- Services requested.
- Pricing guideline used.
- Assumptions.
- Items or conditions that could change the final price.
- Whether management review is needed.

For Illinois household goods moves, confirm Dos Manos is using required approved estimate forms and tariff-compliant pricing where applicable. The working compliance rule is that Illinois intrastate household goods moves need an ICC-compliant written estimate before service starts, Consumer Guide confirmation, bill of lading/freight bill, valuation election, inventory when required, and required vehicle/cab-card documentation as applicable.

## Supermove Entry Process

Every lead should be entered or reported with enough detail for management to track it.

Required lead fields in Supermove or the approved record:

- Customer name.
- Phone.
- Email.
- Pickup address / ZIP.
- Delivery address / ZIP, if known.
- Move date or target window.
- Move type.
- Lead source.
- Referring person or partner, if any.
- Assigned operator / participant.
- Lead status.
- Notes on job size/scope.
- Next follow-up date.

Required before booking:

- Written estimate completed/sent.
- Estimate accepted.
- Crew/truck plan.
- Pricing/rate/minimum confirmed.
- Inventory/scope notes.
- Specialty items.
- Access issues.
- Deposit/payment terms.
- Customer documents sent/signed.
- Source/attribution confirmed.
- Pricing exception approval, if applicable.

If the participant cannot enter Supermove directly, they must notify management the same day with the same information.

## Supermove Quality Standard

A useful Supermove record should let another manager or crew member understand the job without calling the participant for basic facts.

| Field area | Standard |
| --- | --- |
| Customer contact | Name, phone, and email if available. |
| Move details | Date, origin, destination, move type, inventory or scope, and access notes. |
| Pricing | Pricing guideline, assumptions, and conditions that may change the final cost. |
| Source | How the customer heard about Dos Manos, referral source, territory channel, or ad/source tag. |
| Follow-up | Quote status, follow-up date, and last customer contact. |
| Risk notes | Fragile, heavy, sentimental, high-value, or difficult items; building rules; protection needs. |
| Issue notes | Customer concerns, price confusion, claims, damage, or escalation history. |

If it is not in Supermove or the approved record, management may treat it as not tracked.

## Lead Tracking Process

Always ask:

> How did you hear about Dos Manos?

Record the answer. If the answer is a person, record the person's name and relationship if appropriate. If the answer is online, record the page, ad, social account, search term, or territory channel if known.

Lead tracking matters because it affects follow-up, revenue share, territory attribution, and future marketing decisions.

Operator Track source tags:

- `OPTRACK_[ParticipantName]`
- `OPTRACK_[ParticipantName]_REFERRAL`
- `OPTRACK_[ParticipantName]_PERSONAL`
- `OPTRACK_[ParticipantName]_FOLLOWUP`

Referral tags:

- `REF_[PartnerName]`
- `REF_REALTOR_[PartnerName]`
- `REF_PM_[PartnerName]`
- `REF_SENIOR_[PartnerName]`
- `REF_CONTRACTOR_[PartnerName]`

If the lead is not entered on time, the default rule is that it is not eligible for revenue share unless management can verify the lead source before the job is booked or completed. Repeated failure to enter leads counts against Supermove discipline and promotion readiness.

## Customer And Company Information

Customer and company information should be handled carefully.

Do:

- Keep customer names, phone numbers, addresses, job details, pricing notes, and payment issues inside approved Dos Manos tools and communication channels.
- Share job information only with people who need it to quote, schedule, perform, bill, review, or resolve the job.
- Treat referral partner lists, territory plans, pricing guidelines, and customer records as company information.
- Escalate if customer information is sent to the wrong person or handled improperly.

Do not:

- Keep separate private customer lists.
- Move Dos Manos leads outside the approved process.
- Use customer or referral partner information for personal arrangements.
- Share customer details casually with people outside the job.

## Customer Follow-Up Process

Follow up with open quotes until the customer books, declines, or becomes stale under management's rules.

Each follow-up should:

- Be short.
- Be helpful.
- Restate the next step.
- Avoid pressure.
- Be logged in Supermove or the approved tracking process.

Suggested cadence unless management sets another rule:

| Timing | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Same day as quote | Confirm the customer received the quote and knows the next step. |
| 1 business day later | Ask whether they have questions or want to reserve the date. |
| 3-4 business days later | Check whether timing, scope, or decision status has changed. |
| 7 days later | Send a final helpful follow-up unless the customer asks to continue. |

Do not harass the customer. If the customer says no, record the reason if appropriate and close the follow-up.

## Cancellation Or Reschedule Handling

If a customer cancels or asks to reschedule:

1. Stay calm and helpful.
2. Confirm the job, date, and customer.
3. Ask whether they are canceling, rescheduling, or still deciding.
4. Record the reason if the customer is willing to share it.
5. Update Supermove or notify management immediately.
6. Do not promise deposit treatment, refund treatment, fee waiver, or priority rescheduling unless management approves it.
7. If the job came from a referral partner or territory channel, record the status clearly for attribution and follow-up.

## Referral Partner Outreach Process

Good referral partners may include realtors, property managers, senior living contacts, contractors, decorators, storage facilities, estate sale companies, and repeat customers.

Outreach should be professional and local. The goal is to build trust, not ask for favors.

Record every referral contact in Supermove, the affiliate/referral tracker, or the weekly report.

Dos Manos may pay approved referral partners or affiliates, generally 5% of the referred moving job after Dos Manos is paid by the customer. Do not promise referral fees unless management has approved the partner, tracking source, W-9 process, and payout terms.

Preferred plain-language referral line:

> We pay you 5% for every moving job you refer to us, after Dos Manos is paid by the customer.

For partner leads, aim for same-business-day local quote response, COI turnaround under 24 hours when required and practical, proactive status texts, same-day issue acknowledgment, and resolution within one business day where practical.

Suggested referral partner follow-up cadence:

| Timing | Action |
| --- | --- |
| First contact | Explain Dos Manos, the quality standard, and the approved referral setup process. |
| 2-3 business days later | Follow up with a short note or call if they showed interest. |
| After first referred lead | Confirm the lead was received and handled through the approved process. |
| After completed referred job | Thank the partner if appropriate and confirm management/accounting will handle any approved payout. |
| Monthly | Check in only if the relationship is active or there is a useful reason to connect. |

Do not spam referral partners. The goal is to be useful and reliable.

## Review Request Process

Ask for reviews after a successful job.

A good review request is:

- Timely.
- Short.
- Personal.
- Based on real customer satisfaction.
- Never pressured.
- Never tied to an improper incentive.
- Never written by Dos Manos on behalf of the customer.
- Never limited only to customers who are expected to leave positive feedback, unless management has approved a compliant review process.

## Job Leadership Standards

When leading or helping lead a job:

- Review the job notes before arrival.
- Confirm scope with the customer.
- Walk the job before moving items.
- Identify fragile, high-value, difficult, or unusual items.
- Protect floors, walls, doors, and furniture as required.
- Set the pace without sacrificing care.
- Communicate with the crew.
- Keep the customer informed.
- Report scope changes, damage, crew issues, or customer concerns immediately.
- Finish with a customer walkthrough when possible.

## End-Of-Job Checklist

Before the crew leaves, confirm:

- [ ] Customer walkthrough completed when practical.
- [ ] Added scope, extra time, or changes were communicated and documented.
- [ ] Damage or possible damage was documented and escalated.
- [ ] Customer issue or dissatisfaction was escalated.
- [ ] Payment status or collection issue was reported.
- [ ] Review request was made only if appropriate under company process.
- [ ] Supermove/job notes were updated or sent to management.

## Safety And Vehicle Standards

Operators are not mechanics or compliance officers, but they are responsible for taking safety seriously and escalating problems.

Before or during jobs, watch for:

- Unsafe driving conditions.
- Driver qualification or assignment concerns.
- Truck defects or equipment problems.
- Missing or damaged moving equipment.
- Unsafe lifting or carrying.
- Rushing that creates injury or damage risk.
- Job-site hazards.
- Customer access conditions that create safety problems.
- Incidents, citations, or near misses that management needs to know about.

Report vehicle defects, safety incidents, citations, injuries, customer-property damage, and equipment problems through the approved Dos Manos process. Do not keep safety issues informal.

For heavy, awkward, or repetitive moving tasks, use the right equipment and crew support. Do not force a lift just to save time. Awkward distance from the body, twisting, poor handholds, stairs, and repetition increase risk and should change the plan.

## Crew Communication Standards

The crew should know:

- Job scope.
- Customer expectations.
- Access issues.
- Specialty items.
- Protection requirements.
- Time concerns.
- Who is speaking with the customer.
- When to stop and escalate.

Do not argue with crew members in front of the customer. Correct safety, damage, or service issues quickly and professionally.

## Damage Prevention Basics

Before moving items:

- Check pathways.
- Protect floors and doors where needed.
- Protect walls, railings, elevators, and customer property where needed.
- Wrap furniture properly.
- Use the right equipment.
- Do not drag items across floors.
- Do not rush tight turns, stairs, or heavy pieces.
- Ask for help on difficult pieces.
- Report pre-existing damage before moving the item when possible.
- Photograph visible pre-existing damage when management policy or job conditions call for it.

## Customer-Property Protection Standards

Use the protection needed for the job, building, weather, and customer expectations.

| Area | Standard |
| --- | --- |
| Floors | Use appropriate floor protection when needed for weather, traffic, delicate surfaces, or building requirements. |
| Walls and corners | Slow down in tight turns and protect high-risk paths when needed. |
| Doors and railings | Watch contact points and protect or pad when risk is visible. |
| Elevators | Follow building rules and use elevator protection when required or appropriate. |
| Furniture | Wrap, pad, or protect items based on material, risk, and move conditions. |
| Fragile or sentimental items | Confirm handling expectations and escalate if special approval or equipment may be needed. |
| Pre-existing damage | Document visible issues before moving when practical. |
| Weather | Adjust protection and communication for rain, snow, ice, mud, or heat. |

If the job needs protection that the crew does not have, pause and escalate before creating avoidable damage.

## Photo And Walkthrough Documentation

Use photo documentation when it helps protect the customer, the crew, and Dos Manos.

Document:

- Visible pre-existing damage.
- Tight access points.
- Specialty or high-risk items.
- Completed protection work when helpful.
- Damage or possible damage.
- Job completion condition when appropriate.

Perform a customer walkthrough before moving items when practical. Perform an end-of-job walkthrough when appropriate so the customer can confirm major items and spaces before the crew leaves.

## Customer Issue Escalation

Escalate immediately if:

- There is an injury, truck accident, or police involvement.
- There is major property damage or high-value item damage.
- The customer disputes the price.
- The customer says the quote was misleading.
- The scope is larger than expected.
- There is damage or possible damage.
- The customer is angry or losing trust.
- The customer threatens legal action, an ICC complaint, a chargeback, or a public review blast.
- There is a missed pickup, payment refusal, crew conflict, or insurance issue.
- Crew safety is at risk.
- The job may need more labor, time, truck space, or management approval.
- The customer requests a certificate of insurance or additional insured document.
- The job may cross state lines or require federal authority / FMCSA / U.S. DOT compliance review.

Do not make private deals, admit fault, promise payment, interpret insurance, waive charges, or promise claim outcomes. Say: "We'll document it and get it to management so it can be handled through the company's claims process."

## COI And Additional Insured Requests

Some buildings, property managers, senior living communities, or commercial contacts may request a certificate of insurance or additional insured documentation.

If this happens:

1. Get the exact building or contact requirements.
2. Ask for the required certificate holder name, address, email, move date, and any sample language.
3. Enter or send the request through the approved Dos Manos process.
4. Do not promise turnaround time unless management has confirmed it.
5. Record the request and status in Supermove or the approved record.

Target response may be under 24 hours where practical, but it is not guaranteed unless management approves the commitment.

Operator-approved insurance language:

> We are licensed and insured for the work we are authorized to perform, and our office can provide the appropriate certificate if needed.

Operators should not issue or modify COIs, quote limits, interpret coverage, or add additional insured language. Central admin/ownership handles COI and additional insured requests through the insurance agent.

## Interstate Move Requests

If a customer asks about a move crossing state lines, pause before quoting or booking.

1. Gather the basic job facts.
2. Tell the customer management needs to confirm the correct authority and process.
3. Escalate to management.
4. Do not represent that Illinois intrastate authority covers the move.
5. Do not book the job until management approves the interstate process.

Known long-distance interest or past discussion does not replace FMCSA / U.S. DOT compliance review.

Working rule: do not book interstate household goods moves unless Dos Manos' FMCSA/U.S. DOT authority and required interstate documents are confirmed. If authority is not confirmed, decline or refer the customer to an authorized mover.

## Issue Severity Guide

Use this guide to decide how quickly to escalate.

| Level | Examples | Required action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Level 1 - routine question | Customer asks about timing, packing, parking, or quote details. | Answer if approved, document in Supermove, and follow up if needed. |
| Level 2 - service concern | Customer is uneasy, quote details are unclear, scope may change, or partner needs status. | Clarify, document, and notify manager if pricing, timing, or scope may change. |
| Level 3 - urgent escalation | Price dispute, possible damage, angry customer, safety concern, crew issue, COI/additional insured request, or major scope change. | Pause, document, contact management immediately, and do not promise outcomes. |
| Level 4 - compliance or major risk | Interstate move question, serious injury, serious property damage, threat of chargeback/legal action, or major brand risk. | Stop improvising, escalate to management/ownership immediately, preserve records, and follow company direction. |

## Service Recovery Process

When something goes wrong, speed and clarity matter.

1. Acknowledge the issue the same day when practical.
2. Get the facts before assigning blame.
3. Document photos, customer statements, crew statements, job notes, and timing.
4. Escalate to management.
5. Give the customer the next clear step.
6. Follow up when management has reviewed the issue.

Do not treat good recovery as a substitute for prevention. Repeated avoidable damage, repeated complaints, or unresolved claims should block promotion until the pattern is corrected.

## Issue Documentation Checklist

When a customer issue, damage concern, claim, payment dispute, or major complaint occurs, document:

- Customer name and job number.
- Job date.
- Item description and location.
- Bill of lading / estimate and inventory if applicable.
- Repair or replacement estimate if available.
- Date and time the issue was reported.
- Who reported the issue.
- What the customer says happened.
- What the crew says happened.
- Photos or videos, if available and appropriate.
- Relevant Supermove notes.
- Item, room, property area, or payment issue involved.
- Whether there was visible pre-existing damage.
- Whether the issue was acknowledged the same day.
- Who in management was notified.
- Next step communicated to the customer.

Do not edit facts to protect yourself or the crew. Clean facts protect the customer, Dos Manos, and the operator.

Formal claims should be routed to Bryan/Operations first, with Alex copied for material claims, payment issues, legal threats, ICC exposure, or insurance exposure. Illinois household goods claim timelines require professional compliance handling; the working reference is at least 90 days for a customer to submit loss/damage claims, written acknowledgment within 30 days, and payment, denial, or written settlement offer within 120 days.

## Scripts

### First Customer Call

> Thanks for calling Dos Manos Moving, this is [Name]. I can help get the details so we can give you the right next step. What date are you looking to move, and what are you moving?

After details:

> A few things can affect the final price, like inventory, stairs, elevators, long carries, packing, and added stops. I will note those clearly so the quote matches the job as closely as possible.

Close:

> How did you hear about Dos Manos? I want to make sure we track that correctly.

### Quote Follow-Up

> Hi [Name], this is [Name] with Dos Manos Moving. I wanted to follow up on your quote for [move date]. Do you have any questions, or would you like us to hold the next step for you?

### Estimate Explanation

> Based on what you shared, this estimate depends on the inventory, access, stairs or elevators, distance, packing needs, and any added stops. I am going to document those details so the office and crew understand the job. If the actual scope changes, the final price may change under Dos Manos guidelines.

### Cheaper Competitor Response

> I understand price matters. Dos Manos is focused on careful crews, clear communication, and protecting your home and belongings. We may not always be the lowest quote, but the goal is for you to feel the job was handled professionally and worth the price.

### Discount Request Response

> I do not want to cut the price in a way that creates a rushed or under-planned move. Let me make sure we understand the scope first. If there is a legitimate adjustment available, I will get it approved rather than guessing.

### Scope Change Response

> That may change the time, crew, truck space, or final price. I am going to document the change and confirm the next step with management so we handle it clearly before the crew keeps going.

### Quality Positioning

> Dos Manos is not trying to be the cheapest mover. We are trying to be the mover customers trust to communicate clearly, protect their property, send prepared crews, and handle the job the right way at a fair price.

### Referral Partner Outreach

> Hi [Name], I am with Dos Manos Moving. We help customers with professional local moving services, and we are building relationships with people who need reliable movers for clients and residents. If you ever have someone who needs careful, professional moving help, I would be glad to be a point of contact.

### Partner-Specific Openers

Realtor:

> A lot of moving stress lands on the agent when the mover is late, careless, or hard to reach. Dos Manos focuses on clear communication and careful crews so your client feels taken care of on moving day.

Property manager:

> We know move-ins and move-outs affect residents, elevators, docks, hallways, and your team. Dos Manos takes building rules and property protection seriously.

Senior living contact:

> Senior moves need patience, communication, and care. Dos Manos can help families who need a mover that treats the move as more than just boxes and furniture.

Contractor, decorator, or flooring contact:

> When your work depends on furniture being moved carefully, you need a mover who protects the property and communicates clearly. Dos Manos can be a reliable contact for those jobs.

### Referral Partner Payment Explanation

> We pay approved referral partners 5% for every moving job they refer to us, after Dos Manos is paid by the customer. We track referrals through our system so the source is clear. I can get you the setup steps if you want to participate.

### Review Request

> Thank you again for choosing Dos Manos. If you have a minute, an honest review would help our team and future customers. I can send the review link if that is convenient.

### Customer Issue Escalation

> I hear your concern, and I want to make sure we handle it the right way. I am going to pause and get management involved so we can review the details and give you a clear answer.

### Active Listening Phrases

Use these when a customer is frustrated, uncertain, or comparing options:

- "It sounds like your main concern is [concern]."
- "Let me make sure I have this right."
- "What would make you feel comfortable with the next step?"
- "What matters most for this move: price, timing, careful handling, or something else?"
- "I do not want to guess. Let me confirm that before I promise anything."

Use the phrase, then pause. Let the customer correct, clarify, or add detail before explaining Dos Manos policy.
