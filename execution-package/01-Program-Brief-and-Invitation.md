## Program Purpose

Dos Manos gives serious operators a path to grow from field work into local business leadership.

The Accelerated Management Program is a selective development track for high-potential movers, drivers, foremen, crew leads, and select outside hires. Participants are trained and tested on booking jobs, communicating with customers, following Dos Manos pricing and service standards, leading crews, tracking leads, and helping build local customer demand.

The program is designed to help Dos Manos build future locations in a controlled way while giving proven people a path beyond ordinary field labor.

## Dos Manos Context

Dos Manos Moving is a Chicago-area moving company with its first operating base in DuPage County. The company serves Chicago, DuPage County, and surrounding suburban markets.

Dos Manos positions itself as The Quality Moving Company. The program should reinforce that position. Participants are not being trained to chase the cheapest price in the market. They are being trained to sell, book, and run careful moving work at a fair price, with trained crews, insured operations, reliable communication, and strong customer-property protection.

Dos Manos serves a broad customer base, including professionals, families, retirees, high-end customers, and smaller practical jobs. A quality job can be a large household move, a high-profile relocation, or a smaller customer need like a treadmill, couch, basement item, or specialty piece. The standard is the same: the customer should feel the job was handled professionally and worth the price.

Simple quality-positioning language:

> Dos Manos is not trying to be the cheapest mover. We are trying to be the mover customers trust to communicate clearly, protect their property, send prepared crews, and handle the job the right way at a fair price.

## Who The Program Is For

This program is for people who already show signs of being credible future operators.

Strong candidates usually show:

- Reliability.
- Professionalism.
- Strong work ethic.
- Good customer judgment.
- Clear communication.
- Leadership potential.
- Ability to follow systems.
- Interest in building something larger than a normal field role.

Entry into the program is selective. Being interested is not enough by itself.

Outside hires may be considered, but prior experience does not skip the Operator Track. Every participant must prove Dos Manos-specific customer judgment, service standard, documentation discipline, and job execution before territory assignment is considered.

## Operator Track

Participants begin in the Operator Track.

The Operator Track is the first proving stage. A participant does not receive a territory on day one. The first test is whether the participant can bring in, book, and help execute quality Dos Manos jobs under the company's standards.

During the Operator Track, the participant works on:

- Booking jobs.
- Speaking with customers.
- Basic estimating.
- Following the Dos Manos Pricing Policy.
- Using Supermove or the approved Dos Manos reporting process.
- Tracking leads.
- Following up with customers.
- Building referral relationships.
- Running jobs in the field.
- Leading crews.
- Protecting the Dos Manos service standard.
- Reporting weekly activity and results.

## Territory Operator Path

After a participant proves the basics and Dos Manos is prepared to expand or advertise a market, Dos Manos may assign a defined territory. Territory assignment is not contingent on a fixed number of completed jobs.

At that stage, the participant becomes a Territory Operator. The Territory Operator helps build local demand, local referral relationships, customer reviews, crew consistency, and local reputation in that market.

The territory is still operated under the Dos Manos central system. Dos Manos continues to control brand standards, the Dos Manos Pricing Policy, Supermove, dispatch, billing and collections, insurance, payroll and compensation processing, customer escalation, claims oversight, marketing standards, and final job approval where needed.

## Advertised Location Concept

Once a territory is assigned, Dos Manos may advertise that market as a separate Dos Manos location before it is operationally separated.

Examples:

- `Dos Manos Moving - Now Serving [Territory]`
- `Dos Manos Moving of [Territory]`

Each Advertised Location should use trackable channels, such as a territory phone number, territory website page, territory quote form, territory social media, local ads, referral partner tracking, and Supermove source tracking.

This lets Dos Manos test local demand before committing full location overhead.

Current location context: Dos Manos calls its primary location Naperville, and the secondary advertised location currently being advertised is River Grove. Future territories should be created using market-fit, operator-readiness, tracking, and operating-capacity guidelines, not a one-time first-territory plan.

## General Manager Path

As a territory grows, a strong Territory Operator may become eligible for a General Manager path.

Early General Managers may still be Working Foremen because field leadership and local management overlap while the company grows. The long-term goal is to create a path where a Working Foreman can eventually become a non-laboring General Manager.

A General Manager is responsible for local revenue growth, job quality, crew leadership, customer communication standards, referral relationships, review performance, local issue resolution, reporting, and protecting the Dos Manos brand.

## Long-Term Location-Level Opportunity

Proven General Managers may later become eligible for a location-level opportunity.

Possible structures may include:

- Cash-flow participation.
- Profit participation.
- Phantom equity.
- Buy-in opportunity.
- Location-level ownership.
- Another approved economic structure.

No ownership, equity, cash-flow participation, profit participation, buy-in right, territory right, or General Manager role is automatic. Any future opportunity depends on performance, location maturity, company approval, and final legal and tax structuring.

## Current Compensation Structure

Participants are paid normally for field work they perform.

In addition, participants may receive 10% of collected revenue on approved jobs.

Future compensation arrangements may change based on role, stage, territory, location maturity, and company policy. Revenue-share payment timing may vary by company process and may use next payroll, month-end, scheduled accounting run, or another CEO-approved payout cycle.

## Approved Jobs During The Operator Track

At the Operator Track stage, an approved job is a job that:

- The participant personally brings in or books.
- Follows the Dos Manos Pricing Policy.
- Follows Dos Manos service standards.
- Is entered into Supermove or reported through the approved Dos Manos process.
- Is completed under the Dos Manos brand.
- Is collected from the customer.

Deposits do not count as collected revenue. Refunds, chargebacks, unpaid balances, claims, major unresolved customer issues, or other collection failures may make a job ineligible for the 10% revenue share.

## What A Participant Must Prove Before Receiving A Territory

Before Dos Manos considers territory assignment, the participant and the company must be ready. Territory assignment is not a job-count formula. The participant must show:

- Reliable activity over time.
- Ability to bring in or book quality jobs.
- Customer communication that protects the brand.
- Pricing discipline.
- Consistent use of Supermove or the approved Dos Manos reporting process.
- Good lead tracking.
- Strong job execution.
- Crew leadership.
- Low avoidable damage or claims.
- Good follow-up habits.
- Referral activity.
- Trustworthiness with customers, crews, and company information.
- Commitment to the Dos Manos quality-over-cheap-pricing position.
- Bryan Abrego's confidence that the participant can represent Dos Manos locally.
- Company readiness to support the territory with tracking, crews, trucks, dispatch, billing, claims handling, and service quality.

Revenue matters, but revenue alone is not enough.

## What The Program Does Not Guarantee

The program does not guarantee:

- Entry into the Operator Track.
- Promotion.
- A territory.
- A General Manager role.
- Ownership.
- Equity.
- Cash-flow participation.
- Profit participation.
- A buy-in right.
- Any specific future compensation structure.

Dos Manos may pause, remove, or reassign a participant or territory based on performance, market needs, operational capacity, company standards, or company judgment.

Pause or removal from the Accelerated Management Program is a program decision. Any separate field work, employment, contractor, payroll, or scheduling question must be handled through normal Dos Manos management and company policy.

## Language To Avoid With Candidates

Do not say:

- "You will get a territory if you do well."
- "Once you book a certain number of jobs, you get a territory."
- "This is basically ownership."
- "This is basically your own local business."
- "You are buying into the company."
- "This territory is yours."
- "You will get 10% on everything in that area."
- "The 10% is guaranteed."
- "We can figure out the legal/tax details later."

Better:

- "Territory assignment is possible after documented performance, judgment, trust, company readiness, and Bryan Abrego approval."
- "The current structure may pay 10% of collected revenue on approved jobs."
- "Any future location-level opportunity would require separate review, approval, and documentation."

## First 30 Days In The Operator Track

The first 30 days should prove whether the participant can operate inside the approved Dos Manos process.

Expected early habits:

- Use Supermove or the approved reporting process consistently.
- Ask customers how they heard about Dos Manos.
- Document job facts before quoting or booking.
- Follow up on open quotes.
- Bring questions to management before guessing.
- Protect price by explaining quality and scope, not by cutting corners.
- Report issues, claims, or customer concerns quickly.
- Submit weekly activity on time.
