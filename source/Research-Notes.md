# Research Notes For Polish Pass

These notes are not standalone program documents. They are drafting inputs for scripts, customer communication standards, referral outreach, and management review.

## Negotiation And Customer Conversation

Useful principles:

- Active listening reduces defensiveness, clarifies misunderstandings, uncovers hidden interests, and builds trust.
- Open questions are better than yes/no or leading questions when the goal is to understand the customer's real concern.
- Follow-up questions signal attention and often produce better information.
- Paraphrasing and calmly naming the customer's concern can slow down conflict and help the customer feel heard.
- Silence can be useful after a hard question, customer objection, or price concern. Do not rush to fill every pause.

Applied to Dos Manos:

- Operators should ask before they argue.
- Operators should restate the customer's concern before explaining policy.
- Operators should protect the price by explaining value, scope, crew quality, and property protection instead of discounting reflexively.
- Operators should escalate issues early rather than trying to win an argument with the customer.

## Persuasion And Referral Trust

Useful principles:

- Persuasion should be ethical and grounded in real value.
- Social proof should be specific and truthful.
- Authority should be demonstrated through professionalism, documentation, training, insurance, and clean process.
- Liking comes from respectful rapport and cooperation, not flattery.
- Commitment works best when the next step is clear and easy.
- Reciprocity in referral relationships should be transparent and documented.

Applied to Dos Manos:

- Use reviews and quality standards as proof, but do not exaggerate.
- Explain that Dos Manos is The Quality Moving Company at a fair price, not the cheapest option.
- Do not fake scarcity, exaggerate availability, or imply false urgency to book a job.
- Referral partner language should be plain: "We pay you 5% for every moving job you refer to us," after management approval and proper tracking.
- Every referral relationship should be tracked in Supermove or the approved referral tracker.

Sources:

- Robert Cialdini, Influence, for ethical use of social proof, authority, commitment, consistency, reciprocity, liking, and scarcity.
- Influence at Work, Cialdini principles of persuasion: https://www.influenceatwork.com/6-principles-of-persuasion/
- Influence at Work, books and publications on Cialdini's principles: https://www.influenceatwork.com/books-and-publications/

## Service Recovery

Useful principles:

- Fast, fair, and specific recovery can restore trust after a service failure.
- Recovery is less reliable after repeated failures, so prevention matters more than heroic cleanup.
- Customers usually need acknowledgment, clear next steps, and timely follow-through before they need a perfect speech.

Applied to Dos Manos:

- Acknowledge the issue the same day when practical.
- Give the customer a clear next step.
- Do not promise claim outcomes in the field.
- Document the issue, photos, customer statement, crew statement, and management follow-up.
- Treat repeated complaints, avoidable damage, or unresolved claims as promotion blockers.

Sources:

- Service Recovery Paradox meta-analysis: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1094670507303012
- Maxham and Netemeyer, repeated failures and service recovery effects: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1509/jmkg.66.4.57.18512

## Review Requests And Testimonials

Useful principles:

- Reviews should come from real customers with real experience.
- Operators should not pressure, script, fake, or selectively manipulate reviews.
- Review requests should be simple and compliant with platform and FTC rules.

Applied to Dos Manos:

- Ask satisfied customers for honest reviews after successful jobs.
- Do not offer improper incentives for positive reviews.
- Do not write reviews for customers.
- Do not suppress negative feedback through threats or pressure.

Sources:

- FTC Consumer Reviews and Testimonials Rule Q&A: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/consumer-reviews-testimonials-rule-questions-answers

## Safety And Manual Handling

Useful principles:

- Moving work creates injury risk through heavy loads, awkward postures, poor handholds, stairs, repetition, pushing, pulling, and carrying.
- Training should emphasize equipment use, crew help, planning the lift, and stopping when the job condition changes.
- Safety issues should be documented and escalated instead of handled informally.

Applied to Dos Manos:

- Operators should treat safety as part of the service standard.
- Operators should report truck defects, equipment problems, incidents, citations, injuries, and near misses.
- Operators should avoid forcing unsafe lifts to protect speed or price.

Sources:

- OSHA ergonomics overview: https://www.osha.gov/ergonomics
- NIOSH Revised Lifting Equation overview: https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/ergonomics/about/RNLE.html
