# Execution Package Build Plan

Source-of-truth build plan for the Dos Manos Moving Accelerated Management Program execution package.

## Purpose

This plan tells agents what to build, what order to build it in, and what each deliverable must accomplish.

The goal is to create the minimum document set needed to operate the Dos Manos Moving Accelerated Management Program without creating unnecessary paperwork.

Use `Company-Facts.md`, `Rollout-Answers.md`, `Finalized-Policy-Decisions.md`, and `Remaining-Completion-Questions.md` to keep the package specific to Dos Manos Moving, including Chicago / DuPage market context, Supermove, QuickBooks, The Quality Moving Company positioning, referral program facts, operational compliance context, approval roles, tracking defaults, locked final policy decisions, and true remaining completion gaps.

Agents should build practical working drafts that Dos Manos can actually use to select qualified candidates, train them to book and run jobs, track their leads, activity, and performance, pay the 10% collected revenue share correctly, assign territories after proof of performance, advertise territories as trackable Dos Manos locations, decide when a territory is ready to become a formal location, and protect Dos Manos legally, operationally, and financially.

## Build Principle

Do not create a standalone document unless it directly supports a decision, payment, training step, territory launch, performance review, or legal/business control.

If a document is mostly explanatory, combine it into another document. If a document is useful later but not needed now, defer it. If a script, checklist, or policy is short, include it inside the larger document where it will actually be used.

## Final Execution Package

The execution package has seven core deliverables:

1. Program Brief and Invitation
2. Candidate Selection Form
3. Revenue Share and Lead Attribution Policy
4. Operator Playbook
5. Operator Performance Tracker
6. Territory Assignment and Launch Packet
7. Formal Location Readiness Packet

## Phase 1 - Required Before First Participant Starts

Build these five documents first, following the package build order:

1. Program Brief and Invitation
2. Candidate Selection Form
3. Revenue Share and Lead Attribution Policy
4. Operator Playbook
5. Operator Performance Tracker

## Phase 2 - Required Only When a Territory Is Assigned

Build the Territory Assignment and Launch Packet only after a participant earns a territory.

## Phase 3 - Required Only When a Territory Is Ready to Become a Formal Location

Build the Formal Location Readiness Packet only after a territory has meaningful activity.

## Agent Drafting Standards

For every deliverable, agents must use direct, practical language, keep documents operational, avoid corporate buzzwords, separate internal-only language from candidate-facing language, avoid overpromising promotion, territory rights, compensation, ownership, or buy-in rights, use Dos Manos terminology consistently, make every checklist usable, make every script short enough to actually use, flag legal, tax, payroll, insurance, or employment issues in a final review section only, and avoid internal debate in the body of the document.

Agents should use `Research-Notes.md` to improve scripts and operating standards, especially customer listening, price objections, referral outreach, and service recovery. Do not turn research notes into standalone paperwork.

Any exception involving pricing, payment, attribution, territory launch, public copy, or readiness should be documented in the relevant execution document. Do not normalize exceptions into the program without management approval.

## Required Terminology

Use: Operator Track, Operator, Territory Operator, Working Foreman, General Manager, Advertised Location, Formal Location, approved jobs, collected revenue, service standard, local customer base, referral relationships, centralized operations, location-level opportunity, cash-flow participation.

Avoid: franchise, unless discussing legal review; ownership promises; profitable revenue; correctly priced work; side deals in public-facing materials; poaching in public-facing materials; small company as a compensation justification; brand weakness language; generic corporate phrases.

## Legal and Policy Review Items

Agents may draft plain-English business terms, but final implementation requires professional review for payroll treatment, 1099 versus employee compensation, commission or bonus classification, wage and hour issues, workers' compensation, insurance implications for advertised locations, claims and refund treatment, customer ownership language, referral partner ownership language, confidentiality, non-solicitation, territory reassignment, future equity, phantom equity, profit participation, or buy-in structures, and avoiding accidental franchise implications.

Do not place unresolved legal debate throughout the documents. Put review items at the end.

## Completion Standard

The execution package is complete when Dos Manos can explain the program to a candidate, accept or reject a candidate, train the candidate, track leads and performance, determine which jobs qualify for 10%, assign a territory, advertise the territory with trackable channels, review whether the territory is ready to become a formal location, and identify legal and accounting items needing professional cleanup.

The package should stay lean until real usage proves another document is necessary.
