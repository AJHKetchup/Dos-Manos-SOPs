# Dos Manos Moving Accelerated Management Program

Source-of-truth program outline for execution package drafting.

## Program Purpose

The Dos Manos Moving Accelerated Management Program is a structured path for high-potential movers, drivers, foremen, crew leads, and select outside hires to become Operators, Territory Operators, future General Managers, and potential participants in the economics of the locations they help build.

The program gives ambitious people a path beyond field labor while helping Dos Manos expand into new markets in a controlled, practical way.

The core purpose is simple:

Train the right people to book, run, lead, and grow quality moving jobs under the Dos Manos standard. Then use that proof to build future Dos Manos locations.

This is a management-development program, not just a compensation plan.

## Program Definition

The program is:

- A selective development track for credible candidates.
- A training and testing system for booking, customer communication, field leadership, and job execution.
- A way to build local demand before committing the full overhead of a standalone location.
- A path from field work into local business leadership.
- A long-term opportunity for proven operators to participate in location-level economics.

The program is not:

- An open trial pool.
- A guaranteed promotion.
- A guaranteed territory.
- A franchise offering.
- An immediate ownership grant.
- A replacement for normal field pay.
- A promise that every participant becomes a General Manager.

## Core Program Flow

### Stage 1 - Candidate Selection

Candidates are selected because they already show signs of being capable future operators.

Ideal candidates show reliability, professionalism, strong work ethic, customer judgment, leadership potential, ability to represent Dos Manos well, ability to follow systems, and interest in building something larger than a normal field role.

This program should remain selective. Entry into the program should feel earned.

Outside hires may be considered, but outside experience does not skip the Operator Track. Every participant must prove Dos Manos-specific judgment, documentation discipline, service standard, and job execution before territory assignment is considered.

### Stage 2 - Operator Track

The candidate enters the Operator Track before receiving a territory.

In this stage, the candidate is trained and tested on booking jobs, speaking with customers, basic estimating, following pricing guidelines, using Supermove or the approved Dos Manos process, tracking leads, following up with customers, building referral relationships, running jobs in the field, leading crews, protecting the Dos Manos service standard, and reporting weekly activity and results.

The candidate does not receive a territory on day one. The first test is whether they can personally bring in, book, and help execute quality Dos Manos work.

### Stage 3 - Territory Assignment

After proving the basics, Dos Manos may assign the participant a defined territory. At this stage, the participant becomes a Territory Operator.

The territory gives the operator a defined market to build, but the business is still operated under Dos Manos' central system. The Territory Operator is responsible for building local demand, relationships, and reputation while Dos Manos maintains control over the core operating structure.

### Stage 4 - Advertised Location

Once a territory is assigned, Dos Manos may begin advertising that market as a separate Dos Manos location.

The location can be advertised externally before it is operationally separated. Public-facing examples may include `Dos Manos Moving - Now Serving [Territory]` or `Dos Manos Moving of [Territory]`.

Each advertised territory should have trackable inbound channels, which may include a separate phone number, dedicated website page, dedicated quote form, territory-specific social media, local ads, referral partner tracking, and Supermove source tracking.

Although the territory is advertised as a local Dos Manos location, operations remain centrally managed until the location is ready to stand on its own.

### Stage 5 - Formal Location

A territory becomes a Formal Location when it has enough operating strength to justify a more separated structure.

A Formal Location should have consistent booked and completed revenue, consistent collected revenue, reliable crew coverage, strong job quality, positive customer reviews, low avoidable damage or claims, consistent local lead sources, a capable Territory Operator or General Manager, dedicated assets or reliable truck access, clean reporting, and enough margin to support location-level management.

### Stage 6 - General Manager Path

Early General Managers will usually still be working foremen because Dos Manos is still growing and field leadership overlaps with local management.

As the location grows, the goal is to create a path where the Working Foreman can eventually become a non-laboring General Manager.

A General Manager is responsible for local revenue growth, job quality, crew leadership, customer communication standards, local referral relationships, review performance, territory reporting, local issue resolution, protecting the Dos Manos brand, coordinating with centralized operations, and helping the location mature into a stable business unit.

### Stage 7 - Location-Level Opportunity

Proven General Managers may become eligible for a location-level economic opportunity.

This path is intended for operators who help build a territory into a real Dos Manos location and continue to meet the company's standards for revenue, quality, leadership, and trust.

The location-level opportunity may be structured as cash-flow participation, profit participation, phantom equity, a buy-in opportunity, location-level ownership, or another approved economic structure.

No participant receives ownership, equity, cash-flow participation, or buy-in rights automatically. Eligibility depends on performance, location maturity, company approval, and final legal and tax structuring.

## Compensation Structure

Program participants are paid normally for field work they perform.

In addition, participants may receive 10% of collected revenue on approved jobs.

Normal field pay and program revenue share are separate. A participant may be paid for field work performed even when a job is not eligible for the 10% revenue share.

This is the current program compensation structure. Future compensation arrangements may change based on the participant's stage, role, territory, location maturity, and company policy.

## Lead Tracking and Territory Attribution

Lead tracking must be simple, clear, and consistent. Dos Manos should always ask customers how they heard about the company or who referred them.

During the Operator Track, participants are responsible for directly tracking the leads they bring in. They must either enter the lead into Supermove directly or notify management that they brought in the lead so it can be tracked properly.

At the Territory Operator stage, attribution is based primarily on where the lead comes from. If a customer comes through a territory-specific phone number, website page, quote form, social media account, ad campaign, or other assigned channel, the job is treated as a territory lead.

## Centralized vs. Local Responsibilities

Dos Manos central operations controls brand standards, pricing guidelines, Supermove, dispatch process, billing and collections, insurance, claims oversight, payroll and compensation processing, customer escalation, marketing standards, territory assignment, territory reassignment, job approval standards, compensation policy, legal and administrative documents.

The Territory Operator is responsible for helping build local leads, customer relationships, referral partner relationships, approved booked jobs, job execution, crew leadership, customer satisfaction, reviews, weekly reporting, local market knowledge, and local reputation.

This structure gives the operator responsibility for local growth while Dos Manos keeps control of the operating system.

## Promotion Criteria

Participants advance based on performance, judgment, and trust. Revenue matters, but revenue alone is not enough.

Promotion criteria include reliability, customer communication, booking ability, pricing discipline, job quality, crew leadership, Supermove usage, follow-up discipline, referral activity, review generation, low damage rate, low complaint rate, coachability, ability to follow the approved Dos Manos process, ability to solve problems without creating larger problems, and trustworthiness with customers, crews, and company information.

Trust should be judged by observable behavior:

- Tells management bad news early.
- Records job facts accurately.
- Does not hide customer issues, damage, claims, or payment problems.
- Does not make side arrangements outside the approved Dos Manos process.
- Does not promise territory rights, ownership, claim outcomes, or special pricing without approval.
- Protects customers, crews, and the Dos Manos brand when nobody is watching.

## Review Cadence

Participants submit a Weekly Operator Report covering leads generated, jobs booked, jobs completed, revenue collected, referral contacts made, customer follow-ups completed, reviews requested, issues or claims, help needed from management, and top priority for the next week.

Management reviews activity level, booked revenue, collected revenue, job quality, customer feedback, crew feedback, Supermove usage, review generation, referral relationship progress, territory progress, and readiness for next stage monthly.

## Pause, Removal, or Reassignment

Dos Manos may pause, remove, or reassign a participant or territory if the participant fails to meet company standards.

Reasons include poor customer communication, repeated job quality issues, failure to follow pricing guidelines, failure to use Supermove or other approved processes, failure to track leads properly, misrepresentation to customers, unapproved arrangements outside the approved Dos Manos process, poor crew leadership, lack of activity, repeated customer complaints, avoidable damage or claims, conduct that harms the Dos Manos brand, or loss of trust.

Territories are assigned by Dos Manos and may be modified, paused, reassigned, or discontinued based on performance, market needs, operational capacity, or company judgment.

## Brand Positioning

Dos Manos positions itself as The Quality Moving Company. Program language should reinforce quality over cheap pricing, fair value over lowest-cost positioning, trained crews, insured operations, careful customer-property protection, and professional communication.

Dos Manos should be framed as quality-focused and value-driven, not luxury-only and not bargain-basement. The company serves a broad customer base, including middle to upper-middle-class professionals, families, retirees, high-end customers, and smaller practical moving needs.

External message:

Dos Manos gives serious operators a path to grow from field work into local business leadership.

Internal message:

Dos Manos builds future locations by finding strong operators, teaching them how to book and run quality jobs, assigning territories once they prove the basics, and giving the best performers a path toward long-term location-level opportunity.

The tone should be direct, practical, professional, respectful, quality-focused, and service-business oriented.

The program should not sound like a franchise pitch, a vague corporate leadership program, a hype-heavy recruiting gimmick, a guaranteed ownership offer, or a generic sales commission plan.
